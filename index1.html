Charminar, located in Hyderabad, India, is an iconic historical monument that serves as a symbol of the city's rich heritage. Built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, it stands at the heart of the old city. This impressive structure showcases a unique blend of Islamic architecture and is renowned for its four grand arches, each facing a cardinal direction.
